Natural Intelligence vs Artificial Intelligence

Natural intelligence is innate to all beings. But will an AI ever need to be considered a being. Is this the goal in the pursuit of strong AI?

The main difference between natural and artificial intelligence is that we may never know with 100% certainty the algorithms at play in a biological system. In contrast artificial intelligence uses algorithms based on a theoretical understanding of biology, consciousness and awareness.

Consciousness could be described as the ability to reason. Whereas awareness could be described as the ability to sense. If this is the case then intelligence could be the interplay between the two. The algorithms that underpin AI technologies thus far may well have developed the ability to be aware if they deal with sensorial input. But can an AI reason? Can an AI understand maths or is it just weak AI like a calculator or a printed circuit board?

If a living being has five senses what senses does an AI have?

Definitions of Natural Intelligence

Natural Intelligence refers to the flexible ability possessed by animals, including humans, to achieve a variety of goals in different contexts based on their rich and varied stream of experiences in the natural world.1
The kind of autonomous intelligence that is realized in the brains of animals and humans to attain in their natural environment goals defined by a repertoire of innate behavioral schemata.2

Definitions of Artificial Intelligence

Study of agents that receive precepts of the environment and perform actions.3
The process of creating computing machines and systems that perform operations analogous to human learning and decision-making.4
